**Chadwick Boseman (1976–2020)**

Chadwick Boseman was an acclaimed American actor, writer, and producer whose talent, dignity, and dedication inspired millions around the world. Born on November 29, 1976, in Anderson, South Carolina, he studied directing before pursuing acting, bringing depth and authenticity to every role he portrayed.

Boseman became widely recognized for playing influential historical figures such as Jackie Robinson in *42*, James Brown in *Get on Up*, and Thurgood Marshall in *Marshall*. He achieved global fame as King T'Challa in Marvel's *Black Panther*, a role that became a cultural milestone and a symbol of pride, leadership, and representation for audiences worldwide.

Beyond his on-screen achievements, Chadwick Boseman was admired for his humility, generosity, and unwavering commitment to his craft. Even while privately battling colon cancer, he continued to deliver remarkable performances, demonstrating extraordinary resilience and strength.

Chadwick Boseman passed away on August 28, 2020, at the age of 43. His legacy lives on through his unforgettable performances, his impact on cinema and culture, and the inspiration he continues to provide to people across generations. He is remembered not only as an exceptional actor but also as a beacon of courage, grace, and hope.
