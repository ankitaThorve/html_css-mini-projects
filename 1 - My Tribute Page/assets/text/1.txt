**Chadwick Boseman (1976–2020)**

*"In my culture, death is not the end. It's more of a stepping-off point. You reach out with both hands, and Bast and Sekhmet lead you into a green veld where you can run forever."* — **T'Challa, *Black Panther***

Chadwick Boseman was an acclaimed American actor, writer, and producer whose talent, dignity, and dedication inspired millions around the world. Born on November 29, 1976, in Anderson, South Carolina, he studied directing before pursuing acting, bringing depth and authenticity to every role he portrayed.

Boseman became widely recognized for portraying influential historical figures such as Jackie Robinson in *42*, James Brown in *Get on Up*, and Thurgood Marshall in *Marshall*. He achieved global fame as King T'Challa in *Black Panther*, a role that became a cultural milestone and a symbol of courage, leadership, and hope for audiences around the world.

Beyond his extraordinary performances, Chadwick Boseman was admired for his humility, generosity, and unwavering commitment to his craft. Even while privately battling colon cancer, he continued to create remarkable work, demonstrating exceptional resilience and strength.

Chadwick Boseman passed away on August 28, 2020, at the age of 43. His legacy lives on through his unforgettable performances, his profound impact on cinema and culture, and the inspiration he continues to provide to generations. As King T'Challa reminded us, *"Death is not the end"*—a sentiment that beautifully reflects how Chadwick Boseman's spirit and influence will endure forever.
